Technology Readiness Levels

Technology Readiness Level (TRL) is a type of measurement system used to evaluate the maturity level of technology. A particular technology project is evaluated using different parameters for each level and depending of the stage of the project’s progress a TRL rating is assigned. This tool is based in nine technology readiness levels.

1. Development

The basic principle has been observed and reported. Scientific research is beginning and those results are being translated into future research and development. Technology concept or application has been formulated. This takes place after the basic principles have been studied and practical applications can be applied to those initial findings.

2. Validation

Experimental proof of concept. Active research and design had begun. Generally both analytical and laboratory studies are required at this level to understand the technology’s viability and feasibility to proceed through the development process. Technology has been validated in lab. Once the proof-of-concept technology is ready, a first initial prototype is validated using multiple component pieces.

3. Prototype

Technology has been validated in a relevant environment. The technology is identified as a breadboard technology and must undergo more rigorous testing than technology that is only at TRL Simulations should be run in environments that are as close to realistic as possible.

4. Pilot & demonstration

Technology has been demonstrated in relevant environment. This technology has a fully functional prototype or representational model. There has been a system prototype demonstration in operational environment.

5. Market introduction

System has been completed and qualified. The technology has been tested and "qualified" and it's ready for implementation and commercialization.

6. Expansion

Actual technology has been proven with success in operational environment. Technology is available for the market.

Investment Readiness Levels

The Investment Readiness Level (IRL) is a visual way to share a common understanding of startup readiness status. This tool allows teams to demonstrate their competence through evidence, which enables investors to make data-driven decisions. The IRL of a team increases as they collect more evidence through customer development and business model validation.

1. Complete first pass canvas

It is important to understand all the components of a business model canvas and to have hypotheses in each section to test. Add at least one business model hypothesis in each of the nine sections of the canvas to achieve this level.

2. Market Size / Competitive analysis

If your market is too small, you have no business, which is why it is crucial to continuously update your market size analysis. Complete the TAM/SAM/TM market sizes with references to achieve this level. Fill in market sizing information on your project page.

3. Problem / Solution validation

Building something no one wants is one of the primary reasons for startup failure. Teams need to ensure that they have a value proposition that addresses a specific customer segment. Through customer development, validate at least one Customer Segment and associated Value Proposition to achieve this level.

4. Prototype Low fidelity MVP

Minimum viable products (MVP) are the best way to really test your Value Propositions. A low-fidelity version is the quickest way to communicate value to a cutomer. Link to a low-fidelity MVP to achieve this level.

5. Product / Market fit validation

You have probably discovered by now that you need to adress multiple Customer Segments, each with unique Value Propositions. For taking Problem / Solution fit to the next level, it is crucial to demonstrate that you are serving a viable market. Validate at least half of the Customer Segments and associated Value Propositions to achieve this level.

6. Righ side of the canvas validation

After achieving Product / Market fit, a team needs to ensure they have a way to deliver value to their customers, a customer aquisition strategy, and a sustainable revenue model. Validate at least half of the Value Propositions, Customer Relationships, Channels and Customer segments hypotheses to achieve this level in each segment.

7. Prototype High fidelity MVP

Now that the core assumptions have been tested and validated, it i time to refine your MVP for more detailing and testing around channels, customer relationships, pricing, etc. Link to a high-fidelity MVP to achieve this level.

8. Rest of the canvas validation

With Product / Market in place, teams need to think through the logistical and money making aspects of the company. Ignoring these aspects of the business model can lead to some awful surprises. Validate at least half of the hypotheses in all areas of the canvas to achiev this level.

9. Metrics that matter validation

Teams need to demonstrate an understanding of their ecosystem and that their metrics create a compelling story. Complete all the metrics that matter and recieve approval from instructors to achieve this level.
